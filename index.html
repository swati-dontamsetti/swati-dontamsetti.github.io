    Philosophy

    As a teacher, my passion is telling a clear and concise story about the world that is rooted in facts and data. So that, through a proper understanding, I can work collaboratively to affect meaningful change. As humans we make assumptions about the world around us that we believe to be the truth. As the late, great, David Foster Wallace said, "The only thing that’s capital-T True is that you get to decide how you’re gonna try to see [the world]." You can decide that your assumptions, regardless of evidence, are the truth. Or you can ask questions, gather data, and then delve deep into the minutiae to see what story the facts are telling you. I will always seek to do the second. This mindset has made me a curious and life-long learner that is always seeking to understand the story of our world. I am dedicated to uncovering the truth as supported by data, and I am hardworking and uncompromising in pursuit of that goal.
